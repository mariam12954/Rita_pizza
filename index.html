Where Was Pizza Invented?

Italy may be where pizza was invented, but it wasn't until the Napoleons crossed the Atlantic that this cheesy cuisine began to take off. The first documented pizzeria, Lombardi's, opened in Manhattan in 1905 and remains open today. Before that, there were many unlicensed vendors selling steaks to hungry Italian Americans. After World War II, the world began demanding all things American, sending pizza into the stratosphere. Chefs around the world are beginning to experiment with this centuries-old creation, making it new again with local flavors and ingredients like Sicilian-style pizza. No longer considered an outdated meal, pizza became an instant classic, launching numerous chains and endless ways to satisfy cravings. The Salerno brothers opened their first pizzeria in 1966 in Chicago, and they've been delighting locals ever since!
